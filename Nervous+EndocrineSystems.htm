Synapse

The presynaptic cell (top) contains many spherical synaptic vesicles, each of which holds neurotransmitters. An action potential activates the voltage-gated calcium channels of the pre-synaptic cell. The entrance of Ca2+ allows synaptic vesicles to bind with the axon terminal membrane and release neurotransmitters into the synaptic cleft. The released neurotransmitters can then bind to receptors on the post-synaptic cell, allowing positive ions to flow in and create an action potential in that cell.
